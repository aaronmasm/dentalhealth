Specialties

Learn more about our specialties.

Aesthetic Dentistry

A beautiful smile is a powerful personal tool. Whether through teeth whitening or porcelain veneers, we can help you achieve a smile that will open many doors.

Aesthetic Dentistry also focuses on replacing old amalgam fillings with resin restorations.

Learn More

Periodontics

Periodontics involves the diagnosis and treatment of issues with the supporting tissues of the teeth, such as gums and the underlying bone (gingivitis and periodontal disease).

Learn More

Oral Rehabilitation

Oral Rehabilitation focuses on restoring function and aesthetics in cases where teeth have been lost due to decay or periodontal disease. Treatments include fixed (crowns, bridges) and removable (dentures) prostheses.

It also addresses issues related to temporomandibular joints and bruxism.

Learn More

Implants

The best alternative for replacing lost teeth is dental implants. Through a simple, painless procedure, these artificial roots are inserted into the remaining bone to reconstruct the external part of the tooth, restoring function and aesthetics.

Implants offer the advantage of eliminating the need to wear down neighboring teeth as with fixed bridges.

Learn More

Endodontics

When the inner part of the tooth has been infected due to deep decay, endodontic treatment involves cleaning and sealing the dental canals to save the affected tooth. The external portion of the tooth can then be reconstructed.

Learn More

Orthodontics

This specialty focuses on diagnosing and treating misalignments of the teeth and/or jawbones. Typically, the treatment involves placing brackets and thin wires to align the teeth and adjust the bite. Ask about Invisalign and Invisible Orthodontics (no brackets!).

Learn More

Pediatric Dentistry

This specialty covers the care of patients from prenatal stages to the final year of schooling. Its main focus is prevention, ensuring children reach adulthood with healthy, aligned teeth through proper guidance.

Learn More

Oral Surgery

The most common surgical interventions in Dentistry include tooth extractions, whether for damaged teeth that are not viable for reconstruction or third molars (wisdom teeth).

Learn More

Comprehensive Dentistry

It Encompasses common dental treatments, such as scaling, prophylaxis, and restorations.
